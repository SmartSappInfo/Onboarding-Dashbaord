# 🧩 1. Insurance Firms

### Core datasets

* **Policyholder data**

  * Name, age, contact info, beneficiaries
* **Policy data**

  * Policy type (life, auto, health), coverage, premiums, renewal dates
* **Claims data**

  * Claim history, amounts, status, fraud flags
* **Risk profiling**

  * Health records, driving history, risk scores

### Loyalty & preferences

* Preferred communication (SMS/email/agent)
* Renewal behavior
* Product preferences (e.g., life vs health bundles)
* Loyalty tier (long-term customers, multi-policy discounts)

# 🔁 Cross-cutting datasets (used by almost ALL)

These are important if you're designing a CRM:

### 1. Loyalty Program Tracking

* Points earned/redeemed
* Tier levels (e.g., Gold, Platinum)
* Rewards history
* Referral activity

### 2. Client Preferences

* Communication channel (SMS, WhatsApp, email)
* Contact timing (morning, weekends, etc.)
* Product/service interests
* Language & localization preferences

### 3. Interaction Timeline (very important)

* Every call, email, meeting
* Notes and outcomes
* Next follow-up actions

# 🧠 Key Insight (for building systems)

Even though industries differ, most CRM datasets fall into 5 universal layers:

1. **Identity** → Who the customer is
2. **Relationship** → Interactions & communication
3. **Transactions** → Money, purchases, deals
4. **Behavior** → Usage, engagement, patterns
5. **Preferences & Loyalty** → What they like + how to retain them
